Health Information

Diabetes Mellitus (DM)

Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs when the pancreas is no longer able to make insulin, or when the body cannot make good use of the insulin it produces.

Symptoms:

  • Increased thirst and frequent urination
  • Extreme hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores

Management and Prevention:

  • Maintain a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables and whole grains
  • Exercise regularly (at least 30 minutes daily)
  • Monitor blood sugar levels regularly
  • Take prescribed medications as directed
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption
  • Maintain a healthy weight

Hypertension (HTN)

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high.

Symptoms (often none until severe):

  • Headaches
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nosebleeds
  • Dizziness
  • Chest pain
  • Visual changes

Management and Prevention:

  • Reduce salt intake
  • Eat a diet rich in fruits, vegetables and low-fat dairy
  • Exercise regularly
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Quit smoking
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques
  • Take prescribed medications consistently

Hepatitis B Virus (HBV)

Hepatitis B is a serious liver infection caused by the hepatitis B virus (HBV). For some people, hepatitis B infection becomes chronic.

Symptoms:

  • Abdominal pain
  • Dark urine
  • Fever
  • Joint pain
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Weakness and fatigue
  • Yellowing of skin and eyes (jaundice)

Prevention and Control:

  • Get vaccinated (HBV vaccine is highly effective)
  • Practice safe sex using condoms
  • Avoid sharing needles or personal items like razors/toothbrushes
  • Ensure sterile equipment for tattoos/piercings
  • Pregnant women should be tested for HBV
  • For chronic cases, regular monitoring and antiviral therapy as needed

General Disease Prevention

Preventive measures can significantly reduce your risk of developing many diseases.

Key Prevention Strategies:

  • Vaccination: Stay up-to-date with recommended vaccines
  • Healthy Diet: Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains and lean proteins
  • Regular Exercise: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly
  • Weight Management: Maintain a healthy weight
  • Stress Reduction: Practice relaxation techniques like meditation
  • Regular Check-ups: Early detection through screenings is crucial
  • Hygiene Practices: Wash hands frequently and practice food safety
  • Avoid Tobacco: Don't smoke and avoid secondhand smoke
  • Limit Alcohol: Drink in moderation if at all
  • Safe Sex: Use protection to prevent STIs
